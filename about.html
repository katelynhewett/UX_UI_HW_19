About Me

It’s been a long road home.

I earned my Bachelor’s degree in English and French, briefly interned as an editor for an architect and urban planning blog, and spent 6 years working in real estate sales and marketing...so how in the world did I end up in UX design?
Great Question.

It’s all about the story for me. Language is one vehicle for sharing stories and processing life. Home is the origin of so many of these stories. And today, we also experience our lives through the digital spaces and products that help us achieve our goals.

I want to expand my work through UX design to influence the broader range of human story. I bring my editor’s eye to detail, the client-focused lens and passion for translating complex ideas honed through my real estate work, and my neverending love of story to the table.

Let’s tell a tale.

User Research

The origin of my research. I’m always asking myself “why?” which helps me to dig deeper into the questions that drive user behavior.

2. Empathy and Insight

I use my curiosity to craft research plans that truly empathize with the user, focusing on their goals and motivations. This allows me to seek out the insights companies need to make their products excel.

3. Story-Driven

My language background and experience working with diverse clients helps me to transform the research into clear and compelling stories that drive action.
